# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We want the rules_elixir_mix community to be rigorous, candid, and welcoming.
People should be able to challenge a design, report a failure, ask a basic
question, or propose a different ownership boundary without being attacked or
dismissed.

This code applies to issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions about the
project, release coordination, and other spaces where someone represents the
project.

## Expected behavior

- Discuss the technical claim, evidence, and tradeoff—not the person's motives
  or ability.
- Be specific and actionable in reviews.
- Respect different levels of Bazel, Erlang, Elixir, native-toolchain, and
  cryptographic experience.
- Acknowledge uncertainty and correct mistakes openly.
- Protect private reports, credentials, personal information, and embargoed
  vulnerability details.
- Accept a maintainer decision gracefully after the relevant evidence has been
  considered.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, or deliberately
  demeaning language.
- Personal attacks, repeated bad-faith argument, trolling, or disruption.
- Publishing another person's private information or correspondence without
  permission.
- Pressuring someone to disclose credentials, identity, employment, location,
  or other personal details.
- Retaliation against a reporter, contributor, reviewer, or maintainer.
- Knowingly misrepresenting test results, security properties, certification,
  provenance, or project support.

## Reporting and enforcement

For conduct concerns, email `support@abilisoft.com` with subject
`rules_elixir_mix conduct`. Do not open a public issue containing sensitive
details. Reports will be handled as privately as practical and shared only with
people needed to investigate and respond.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock conversations, issue a
private warning, impose a temporary participation limit, or permanently remove
someone from project spaces. Responses should be proportionate to severity,
impact, history, and willingness to correct the behavior. A maintainer involved
in a report should not make the final enforcement decision when another
qualified maintainer is available.

Good-faith disagreement, a rejected proposal, or a request for stronger
evidence is not itself misconduct.
